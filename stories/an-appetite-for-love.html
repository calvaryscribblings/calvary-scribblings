Poetry

An Appetite for Love

By Ufedo Adaji · Mar 19, 2026
← Back to Poetry

a collection of poems

Contents

  • 1. Sour
  • 2. Salty
  • 3. Sweet
  • 4. Savoury
Sour
Your love, to me, is like alcohol Sweet poison. Each drink, an attempt to set fire to my feelings and deepest musings. To burn it down. After every punch and kick, when you plant a kiss on my lips, It numbs me, whilst you slowly destroy me.
Your love, to me, is like medicine I don't want it but I need it to survive. Bruises, gashes, fractures and even broken ribs I'd rather suffer that than a broken heart. The side effects are toxic but that's the only type of love I know.
Your love, to me, is an addiction. An unhealthy habit. One that I know will lead me six feet under. Loving you was my only blunder.
Salty
My money is our money But your money is your money. Your pain is our pain But my pain is my pain.
You say I'm meant to be strong for the both of us Then you laden me with heavy responsibility Turning a blind eye to my wobbly knees.
It's one-sided. Not an LLC or PLC but a sole proprietorship Yet you claim we're partners.
Every day, you give us a different name. When you're needy you call it a relationship When you're tired you say it's a friendship. When you're horny you say it's a situationship But in all actuality, you are like a flea Feeding on me, on my emotions and all my vulnerabilities.
You don't see me for me, you don't love me Yet, somehow you need me to satisfy your lust.
In the end, I finally realise. There is no US, there never was.
Sweet
I crave love. I'm not talking about the usual butterflies in the belly cliché kind of infatuation Or the juvenile crush during adolescence. Real love.
The type that brightens a gloomy day with just one phone call. The type that cracks cheesy dry jokes to make me smile. The type that helps me charge my phone when I fall asleep And places the duvet over me when I roll away from it. The type where time fades when we are immersed in a conversation. The type where the simple things make the greatest impact. The type that fills me with peace.
One day, I desperately hope to meet the one. The one person that indulges me without hesitation, No matter how weird or dangerous my ideas are. The one person that helps me build, That stretches a hand to lift me when I fall. The one person that compliments me and my quirkiness. The one person that makes me feel unashamed to drop my mask And let them see me — the real me.
I crave sweet, sweet love.
Savoury
In the end, When all is said and done And my time on Earth is done Let it linger on Like the aftertaste of garlic resting on your tongue.
For a love so strong is meant to last long Long after the corruption of the body And the departure of souls to where they rest Love like ours should stand the test of time In life and the afterlife.
But now as we live, My love for you will never die It may dwindle in the face of life's perils Yet it will forever remain constant.
And in another world, where our souls cross paths I will love you all over again With every beat of my heart.
